Accomplishments & Contributions

Quantum Theory of Light

Einstein’s quantum theory of light proposed that light is composed of small packets of energy called photons that have wave-like properties. In this theory he also explained the emission of electrons from some metals they’re struck by lightning – this was called the photoelectric effect.

Special Theory of Relativity

This Einstein theory helped to explain that time and motion are relative to their observers, as long as the speed of light remains constant and natural laws are the same throughout the universe. Changing our understanding of the universe. It's a scientific theory of space and time

E=mc2

He demonstrated the link between mass and energy that led to the nuclear energy today. Mass-energy equivalence entails that the total mass of a system may change, although the total energy and momentum remain constant.: (Energy = mass times the speed of light squared)

Brownian Movement

This could by far be the best Albert Einstein discoveries, where his observation of the zigzag movement particles in suspension, helped to prove the existence of atoms and molecules. And we all know how fundamental this discovery is to almost every branch of science today.

General Theory of Relativity

Einstein proposed that gravity is a curved field in the space-time continuum created by the existence of mass.General relativity predicted many phenomena years before they were observed, including black holes, gravitational waves, gravitational lensing, & the expansion of the universe.

Manhattan Project

Albert Einstein created the Manhattan Project, a research supported by the U.S., that led to the development of the atomic bomb in 1945. However, after learning about it's destruction in Japan during World War 2, Einstein was campaigning for a ban on nuclear weaponry.

Einstein’s Refrigerator

This may be one of the least known inventions that Einstein is famous for today. Einstein developed a refrigerator design that used ammonia, water, and butane, and required almost no energy to work. Companies can develop this concept further in the near future.

The Sky Is Blue

Albert Einstein wrote in 1911 that relativity was needed to fully understand why the sky is blue. However, the sky is blue because of a process called scattering, which occurs when light hits particles in the air, is absorbed, and then re-emitted. Einstein helped put this argument to rest.

The Laser

By imagining electrons being forced into unstable orbits in an atom, Einstein figured out how energy could be released by atoms. He helped pave the way for the invention of lasers. Scientists can now make the electrons in atoms all emit the same kind of energy in a narrow beam.



Challenges




  • Cosmological Constant: Einstein added this to his equations for a static universe.
  • Static Universe: He believed the universe was unchanging and needed balance against gravity.
  • Expansion Discovery: Hubble showed the universe is expanding, prompting Einstein to reconsider.
  • Removing It: He called the constant his "biggest blunder" and removed it from his equations.
  • LegacyThe constant is now linked to dark energy and cosmic expansion.
  • Scientific Humility: Einstein’s mistake highlights that all scientists can learn and grow.
